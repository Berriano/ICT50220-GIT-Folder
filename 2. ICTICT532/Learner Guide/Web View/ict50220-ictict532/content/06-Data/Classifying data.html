When you get information from various sources including staff and clients, you need to classify the type of data it is. If you are getting information just for a simple project like a phone book that has publicly available information, this information would not be classified as sensitive and therefore available for public use.

However, if the information you are collecting is attached to financial or health information, then the information you are collecting would be classified as sensitive. The reason for this is because financial information could contain tax records such as tax file numbers and health information could contain data relating to a person's mental or physical problems. Both these types of information are protected under Australian Privacy law.

As mentioned previously in the 'Personally, Identifiable Information' section, personal information would be classified as sensitive information and should be treated accordingly.

Apart from the above being classified as sensitive, intellectual property (IP) can also be classified a sensitive. Intellectual property is property knowledge, or also known as the property of your mind. This can be design, trademark, an invention, a brand, or even the application of your idea.

Although the privacy act does not include intellectual property you should always consider if the data you are handling is sensitive or not.
