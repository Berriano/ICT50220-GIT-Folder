Should my privacy policy include 'cookies'? - Yes

A business privacy policy should include a 'cookie' notification section if you are gathering information via a web site.

What is a 'cookie'? A 'cookie' is a small file located in your web browser that gathers data about your web browsing behaviour. Even though 'cookies' do not access information stored on your computer or anything personal, they do allow collection of identifiable information. Use of 'cookies' allows a website to customise its structure to your needs and likings, as well as possibly serve ads to you while you are browsing the internet.

If you have a website that collects personal information, you need to state the way in which the information is collected.

Web analytics

Businesses will use a web analytics application to collect data about your interaction with their website. The sole purpose of collecting personal data in this way is to improve the users experience when visiting the site. Select the checkboxes to see the type of information collected.

Note

If your web browser has Do Not Track enabled, the web analytics application will not track your visit. The web analytics application has to be hosted within Australia, if hosted overseas the third-party must guarantee the same privacy conditions that are met by Australian law.

Email lists, registrations, and feedback

A business will collect information that is provided to them when a user signs up to mailing lists, registers for events, or when submitting feedback on their experience with the business's website. There are applications that manage mailing lists and event registrations. These application vendors will also have a privacy policy that should be linked.

When subscribing to a mailing list, you will be asked to give your express consent that the managing application may use your data for analytics purposes. Analytics are performed when you click on links in the email, or when you download the images in the email. This includes which emails you open, which links you click, your mail client (e.g., 'Outlook" or 'iPhone'), if the action occurred on 'mobile' or 'desktop', and the country geolocation of the IP address (the IP address itself is not stored).
