What is the NIST?

Founded in 1901, the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) is now part of the United States Department of Commerce; they are one of the USA's oldest physical science laboratories. The United States Congress created the agency to remove a serious challenge to U.S. industrial effectiveness at the time, which was a mediocre measurement infrastructure that trailed behind the abilities of Germany, the United Kingdom, and other economic competitors.

Nowadays, NIST measurements provide for the smallest of technologies to the largest and most complicated of human-made innovations. From nanoscale mechanisms so tiny that tens of thousands can fit on the end of a single human hair up to earthquake-resistant buildings and worldwide communication networks.

The Privacy Framework

The NIST Privacy Framework is a voluntary tool developed in cooperation with stakeholders intended to help organisations to develop privacy guidance, tools, and international standards.

NIST has released their version 1 of the Privacy Framework, known as "NIST Privacy Framework: A Tool for Improving Privacy through Enterprise Risk Management". As the title suggests it is a tool to help enhance positive uses of data while protecting individual privacy.
