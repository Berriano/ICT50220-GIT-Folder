Tip

A Privacy Impact Statement (PIA) is a written assessment that identifies the privacy impacts of a proposal and sets out recommendations for managing, minimising, or eliminating those impacts.

570816142 / EKKAPHAN CHIMPALEE / shutterstock.com

A PIA should analyse, assess and describe:

  • the personal information flows in a proposal
  • the potential privacy impacts of those flows
  • the impact the overall project may have on individual privacy
  • how those impacts will be eliminated or minimised.

Information security risk assessment

Tip

You may also need to conduct an information security risk assessment (also known as a threat risk assessment) in conjunction with a PIA. An information security risk assessment is generally more specific than a PIA.

The information security risk assessment:

  • identifies and evaluates security risks (threats and vulnerabilities) and their potential impacts on information handled by an entity.
  • is an iterative process which may be undertaken across your business generally
  • should inform, together with the PIA, the development of risk management and information security policies, plans and procedures.

Note

Once risks have been identified and considering the evolving nature of security risks, regular review and monitoring of information security controls (virtual and physical) should follow. Such reviews should determine if the risk management and information security policies, plans and procedures in place, are adequate in mitigating the risks.

Risk of human error

Human error is regularly claimed as the cause of privacy incidents, so entities should assume that human error will occur and design for it.

See the Department of Immigration and Border Protection: Own motion investigation report(this is an external link and opens in a new window) for an interesting report on how human error resulted in a major breach of personal information.

Strategies to protect personal information

Once your entity has collected and holds personal information, you need to consider what appropriate security measures are required to protect the personal information. This will need to be considered in regard to all of your entity's acts and practices.

Destroy or de-identify personal information

Under APP 11.2, APP entities must also take reasonable steps to destroy or de-identify the personal information they hold once it is no longer needed for any purpose for which it may be used or disclosed under the APPs.
