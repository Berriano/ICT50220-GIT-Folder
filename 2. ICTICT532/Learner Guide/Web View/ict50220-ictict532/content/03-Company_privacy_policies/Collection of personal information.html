Organisations and agencies sometimes need personal information about you to carry out their work. Australian privacy law sets out what personal information they can collect and what they need to tell you.

An organisation may only collect your personal information that is reasonably necessary for their work. An agency may only collect your personal information that is related to their work. They do not need your consent unless the information is sensitive.

Office of the Australian Information Commissioner, 'Collection of personal information' 26/04/2021, CC BY 3.0
