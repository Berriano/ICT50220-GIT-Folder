Backup scheduling is an important part of any preventative maintenance plan.

Most operating system platforms provide a backup and restore tool, and this type of software is also provided by third parties. Traditionally business small businesses just had the option of using a device that allowed them to remove a copy of the data off-site each day. These devices were the USB memory sticks, USB HDD, and backup tape. Now with NBN we have the added benefit of cloud storage or directly operating from a cloud-based application

Cloud based storage

With any cloud-based storage there are two simple ways you can have secure data in regard to backups.

For micro to small businesses, you could just have your files in the cloud and access them from there, this all depends on how reliable your NBN connection is. Most accounting programs for small business, like MYOB, operate in the cloud through a cloud-base application, so your files are already secure, and no backup is required.

Single location storage

You could also have your files stored in a single location.

For example, on a computer, server, or NAS, and then also synchronise with your cloud storage.

For small businesses this can be as simple as having Microsoft 365 subscription that will also give you a 1TB cloud storage, and you just run the OneDrive application to setup what you need synchronised.

No NBN

For areas where the NBN is not reliable, or non-existent.

In this case, a small business is going to rely on the more tradition way of backing up. As in the previous table, the data size this will dictate the way data is backed up. But no matter which device you back up to you are going to need a planned schedule to accomplish this.

Important

When using portable devices for backup, either USB connected or a tape cartridge, it is no good leaving media that is not being used in a drawer next to the backup device. These should be either locked in a fire-proof rated safe or stored off-site with a designated person. Otherwise, if the business has a fire or any other type of destruction incident, then the backup media would be lost with all the rest of the equipment.
