In Australia, the Copyright Act 1968 is the law that governs copyright. In 2000 in Australia the Copyright Amendment (Digital Agenda) Act 2000 was also introduced to better serve the needs of the online environment. In 2006 the Copyright Amendment Bill 2006 was also passed with several aims including:

  • providing stronger rights for copyright owners
  • providing exceptions for consumers to enjoy some copyright material in certain circumstances
  • providing some exceptions for the use of copyright material for socially useful purposes.

Since 2006 there has been an additional 3 amendments and a regulation attached to the Act. These include:

  • Copyright Amendment (Disability Access and Other Measures) Act 2017.
  • Copyright Amendment (Online Infringement) Act 2018.
  • Copyright Amendment (Service Providers) Act 2018.
  • Copyright Regulations 2017.

Duration of copyright

Copyright in Australia is automatic on creation of the work and in general the work is then protected by copyright for a period of 70 years after the end of the year of the creator's death, or 70 years from the end of the year the material was first made public.
