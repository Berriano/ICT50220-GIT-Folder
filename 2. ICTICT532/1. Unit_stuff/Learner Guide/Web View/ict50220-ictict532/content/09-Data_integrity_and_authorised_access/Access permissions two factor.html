Two-factor authentication (2FA) is where you will logon to a system or gain access to an application using two forms of verification. This is done using something you know, such as your password, and something you have, such as a mobile phone.

Two-factor authentication acts as a second line of defence, because if your password is guessed by a hacker, sensitive personal information is now vulnerable to malicious intent. Two-factor authentication attempts to tackle that flaw by requiring a secondary code, called a one-time password (OTP). OTPs are usually six characters in length and produced by a smartphone app before you can gain access to your account. So, if a hacker has your password, they will still need to break a secondary code, which makes getting into the system much more difficult.

There are numerous apps that are now available to setup two-factor authentication, depending on the systems you are using. Two of the largest companies in the IT space, Google and Microsoft, both have apps available, as do many other companies in the marketplace. Check out this video for more information.

Two-factor authentication as a software application is not the only way two-factor authentication can be done, it also can be achieved by hardware. Select the tabs to see the advantages and disadvantages of physical security.

  • Disadvantages

The safest way to lock down your accounts with two-factor authentication is to employ a physical security key. It has been found in studies that security keys blocked 100 percent of bulk phishing and targeted attacks.

Where the is a positive there is normally a negative. The negative of using a security key, is that if you ever break or lose the key you could be locked out of your system, and you will have to change your second-factor authentication method to a new key.
