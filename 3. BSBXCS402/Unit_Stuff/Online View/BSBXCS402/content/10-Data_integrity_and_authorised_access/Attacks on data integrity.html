What is data integrity?

Data integrity is normally referred to the overall accuracy, completeness, and consistency of data. Therefore, data Integrity is a guarantee that the personal information can only be accessed or modified by those who are authorised to do so.

What is a data integrity attack?

Data Integrity is an assurance that the information can only be accessed, or modified, by authorised persons to do so. Therefore, data integrity attacks objectives are to compromise this assurance with the intention to gaining unauthorised access with goals to modify data accuracy and reliability.

An example of a data integrity attack occurred in 2016 – Both the World Anti-Doping Agency and Democratic National Committee are breached with hackers manipulating their data to embarrass the organisations. These types of attacks are the worst modes of cyber-attacks because a manipulated and modified data can have unintended consequences. Imagine if terrorists modify sensitive military and government data. It can lead to catastrophic consequences.

How are data integrity attacks carried out?

Data integrity attacks involve a number of combined attacks on different layers in the system with a purpose to exploit the vulnerabilities. Attackers will achieve a level of accessed based on the level of vulnerabilities. Attackers use a number of tools and techniques to breach and monitor a system over time. Attackers will sometimes imbed their bugs into the infrastructure and wait for years until they think it is the perfect time to attack.

Consider a scenario, in which the attacker plants a malware through phishing emails in the administrator's computer. The malware establishes a key logger program in the system to gain the credentials. In the meantime, he can also change the security settings and infiltrate more harmful programs. This is the way integrity attacks occur. These attacks are very dangerous and have the potential to conquer the whole system.
