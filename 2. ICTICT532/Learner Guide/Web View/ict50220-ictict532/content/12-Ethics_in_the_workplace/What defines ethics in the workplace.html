What is the definition of ethics in the workplace?

Ethics in the workplace is described as the principal code that guides the conduct of employees in respect to what is right and wrong in regard to conduct and decision making.

Workplace ethical decision making takes into account the individual employee's best interest and also takes into account the best interest of those impacted. Ethical behaviour does not only apply to individual employees, but the organisation also should demonstrate standards of ethical conduct.

Why is ethical behaviour in the workplace important?

The presence of an ethical workplace can have many advantages and the absence of ethics can have a negative impact on the workplace.

An organisation that is understood to act ethically by staff can achieve positive benefits and increased business results. The awareness of ethical behaviour can have many benefits.

  • Increased employee performance
  • Job satisfaction
  • Organisational commitment
  • Trust

What are ethics?

In an organisation, management may supply a new employee with documentation about workplace ethics. The documentation itself may come in the form of an employee handbook, or a code of conduct. Whichever form the documentation comes in, there will be a breakdown within the document that has sections on different types of ethical behaviour.

Some organisations may also include the following ethics.

  • Primacy of public interest.
  • Place the interests of the community above those of personal, business, or sectional interests.
  • Enhancement of quality of life.
  • Strive to enhance the quality of life of those affected by your work.
