Tip

The Collins Dictionary defines "Authorised" as officially permitted or empowered.

1404797651 / Rawpixel.com / shutterstock.com

People who are authorised to access sensitive personal information should be properly trained in privacy rights, and how to identify common threats.

Authorised personnel should have a good understanding of the Privacy Act and prior to accessing personal information sign a form indicating the policy and procedures set out in the Privacy Act 1988 and the organisations policies.

An authorised person having access to personal information should have the knowledge of:

  • Principles of the Privacy Act 1988
  • understanding of data protection and encryption
  • how to recognise and action phishing emails
  • how to identify changes in systems and programs that indicate malicious intrusion.

Note

The above should be part of induction for new employees with signed employment agreement.
