Data integrity is an assurance that the information can only be accessed, or modified, by persons authorised to do so. Therefore, data integrity attack objectives are to compromise this assurance with the intention of gaining unauthorised access, to modify data accuracy and reliability

Example

An example of a data integrity attack occurred in 2016 – Both the World Anti-Doping Agency and Democratic National Committee were breached with hackers manipulating their data to embarrass the organisations. These types of attacks are the worst modes of cyber-attacks

Types of attacks

Over time, data has been evolving in the way it is seen in the domain of cyber security. Select the tabs to learn more about types of attack.

  • Data availability
  • Data confidentiality
  • Data integrity.

When data first became easily available, it went through the first type of attacks by malware and distributed denial of service (DDOS). Malware has a history of preventing access to data, while DDOS attacks the bandwidth or resources of a targeted system.

Cyber threats to data confidentiality are characterised as data theft.

Data integrity. This form of data attack does not deny access or steal data like malware. It compromises the integrity of the data’s reliability and accuracy.

How are data integrity attacks carried out?

Data integrity attacks involve a number of combined attacks on different layers in the system with a purpose of exploiting the vulnerabilities. Attackers will achieve a level of access based on the level of vulnerabilities. Attackers use a number of tools and techniques to breach and monitor a system over time. Attackers will sometimes embed their bugs into the infrastructure and wait for years until they think it is the perfect time to attack.

Scenario

Consider a scenario in which the attacker plants a malware through phishing emails in the administrator's computer. The malware establishes a key logger program in the system to gain the credentials. In the meantime, he can also change the security settings and infiltrate more harmful programs. This is the way integrity attacks occur. These attacks are very dangerous and have the potential to conquer the whole system.
