As mentioned earlier, emails and public file sharing services can provide access to the public and therefore the data is unsecured. We've the transmission of data attached to emails, these can be easily hacked during transmission of data packets from the host to the destination.

Many commonly used methods of transmitting data are unsecured.

Receiving unsecure data

There are a number of ways of receiving unsecured data.

  • It can be through emails.
  • Transferring of information from one source to yourself.

Unsecure data can come in many forms, it can be sensitive information that you receive where the sender has not secured the data by encryption or other secure means. This can be by accident, by negligence in the form of not caring, or where the sender is doing it for malicious purposes.

Important

If you receive unsecured data with sensitive information you have to report it to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner as a breach. Breaches of the Privacy Act can have consequences for both the sender and the receiver.

To battle against unsecured data being received by the organisation, you require not only good IT management, but also an industry strength security suite, plus well-trained staff in regard to handling emails, attachments, and data handling.
